Learn how to manage your funds on Owly.

## Depositing Funds

### Supported Assets

Currently, Owly supports:
- **USDC** on Arbitrum network

### How to Deposit

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Deposit Dialog">
    Navigate to your sub-account and click "Deposit".
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy Address">
    Copy the displayed Arbitrum USDC deposit address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send USDC">
    Transfer USDC from your wallet or exchange to this address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for Confirmation">
    Funds typically appear within 1-2 minutes after transaction confirmation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Only send USDC on the Arbitrum network. Sending other tokens or using wrong networks may result in permanent loss of funds.
</Warning>

## Withdrawing Funds

### How to Withdraw

<Steps>
  <Step title="Stop Copy Trading">
    Ensure all copy trading is stopped and positions are closed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Withdraw Dialog">
    Click "Withdraw" on your sub-account card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Amount">
    Specify the amount you wish to withdraw.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Destination">
    Verify your withdrawal address (your connected wallet).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Confirm the withdrawal transaction.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Withdrawals can only be sent to your connected wallet address. This is a security feature to prevent unauthorized withdrawals.
</Note>

## Internal Transfers

You can transfer funds between your sub-accounts without fees.

| Transfer Type | Fee | Time |
|---------------|-----|------|
| Deposit (External) | Network gas only | 1-2 min |
| Withdrawal | Network gas only | 1-2 min |
| Internal Transfer | Free | Instant |

## Balance Overview

Your dashboard shows:
- **Available Balance**: Funds available for trading or withdrawal
- **Position Value**: Current value of open positions
- **Unrealized PnL**: Profit/loss from open positions
- **Total Equity**: Sum of all assets

<Tip>
  Keep some available balance as margin buffer to avoid liquidations during volatile markets.
</Tip>
